How The Internet Works

Browsing the Web

How do we get to google.com?

  1. You: type google.com into search bar
  2. Laptop: asks the ISP (internet service provider) to locate google.com
  3. ISP: asks the DNS (which is like an address book of web locations)
  4. DNS: sends IP Address of google.com to ISP, ISP send this to laptop (your laptop has its own IP Address)

Your laptop now has the IP Address to google.com, now what?

  1. Browser: sends IP Address to Google Servers
  2. Google Servers: responds with HTML/CSS/JavaScript element of google.com

If we type the IP Address of google.com into the search bar, we eliminate the first half of the process.

  1. Type 172.217.7.23 into the search bar
  2. Result: google.com will open.

The Internet Backbone

How do we pass along information accross the network in actuality?

  1. Your home wifi: connected to an Internet modum somewhere on your home
  2. Your home modum: the modum talks with your ISP (internet service provider
  3. ISP: connects to the internet backbone

What is the Internet Backbone?

  • Submarine Cable Map: A connected web of submarine cables that extend accross the globe located at the bottom of the sea floors

Traceroute

Using the terminal, we can trace the process it takes to say search google.com

  1. Open the terminal
  2. Enter traceroute google.com
  3. Your terminal will display the hops taken by your system to retrieve the IP address of google.com
